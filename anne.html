Directed by Moira Walley-Beckett

This reimagining of the classic book and film is a coming-of-age story about a young orphan who is seeking love, acceptance, and her place in the world. Amybeth McNulty stars as Anne, a 13-year-old who has endured an abusive childhood in orphanages and the homes of strangers. In the late 1890s, Anne is mistakenly sent to live with her aging siblings, Marilla and Matthew Cuthbert, who live on Prince Edward Island. Anne, who proves to be uniquely spirited, imaginative, and smart, transforms the lives of Marilla, Matthew, and everyone else in their small town.

Review

The show is well-produced, the acting is good, and the sets are appealing. However, modern media has once again ruined a timeless classic by injecting it with their modern ideas, dilemmas, and social issues. Don't get me wrong; a show about a young girl in the late 1800s dealing with abuse and hardship and all the struggles involved with growing up in that time sounds like a great show. But it is not Anne of Green Gables. This is supposed to be a happy, wholesome show that teaches life lessons. Not this mangled mess. They're just capitalizing on the Anne franchise name. Another big issue is Anne's character. She is so unlikeable, cringey, manipulative, and conniving. That is so wrong. Yes, in the books, Anne lies sometimes and has a temper, but the show could not have gotten her more wrong.
