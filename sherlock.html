Directed by Mark Gatiss and Steven Moffat

In this contemporary version of Sir Arthur Conan Doyle's detective stories, Dr. John Watson is a war vet just home from Afghanistan. He meets the brilliant but eccentric Holmes when the latter, who serves as a consultant to Scotland Yard, advertises for a flatmate. Almost as soon as Watson moves into the Baker Street flat, they are embroiled in mysteries, and Sherlock's nemesis, Moriarty, appears to have a hand in the crimes.


Starring

Benedict Cumberbatch

Review

A brilliantly written and well-acted program. Well done to all concerned. The story had me hooked from the start. All the elements of Holmes are present and correct. So far, most of the original characters have appeared, with hints that more are yet to appear. It is suspenseful and sometimes genuinely funny. I have read all the original stories, and after the late, great Jeremy Brett, I despaired of ever seeing another top-notch Holmes film. This is it. brought up to date for the 21st century, but done with affection and respect for the original. It's nice to see the writers have continued the theme of an intelligent Watson.
