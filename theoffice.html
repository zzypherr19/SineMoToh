A mediocre paper company in the hands of Scranton, PA, branch manager Michael Scott This mockumentary follows the everyday lives of the manager and the employees he "manages." The crew follows the employees around 24/7 and captures their quite humorous and bizarre encounters, as they will do whatever it takes to keep the company thriving.

Steve Carell


John Krasinski

Review

This show is so good and so funny that it actually makes it look like working in an office is fun. It's not just the stars who make this show good either; the supporting cast is all just as funny as the main characters. It was universally thought of as the best sitcom on TV while it was on and is now thought of as one of the best sitcoms in the history of television. The show wasn't quite as good after Steve Carell left, but that's only because the seasons with him were so good. It's still a good show after he leaves; it's just not all-time good like when he was there. I still watch all the reruns when they're on, and they're still just as funny after numerous viewings as they were after one.
