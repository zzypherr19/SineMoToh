Where the Crawdads Sing

Directed by Olivia Newman

Abandoned as a girl, Kya raised herself in the dangerous marshlands of North Carolina. For years, rumors of the marsh girl haunted Barkley Cove, isolating the sharp and resilient Kya from her community. Drawn to two young men from town, she opens herself to a new and startling world. However, when one of them is found dead, Kya immediately becomes the main suspect. As the case unfolds, the verdict as to what happened becomes increasingly unclear, threatening to reveal many secrets.


Starring

Daisy Edga-Jones


Taylor John-Smith


Harris Dickinson


Review

I literally bawled my eyes out at the end and cried in the car all the way home from the theater. This film was very well done, great screen play, great actors, amazing story. In a way I feel like everyone has felt like kya in one way or another. She is brave and strong and I love her! Everything was executed so beautifully and the story lined up in a way that made all my emotions run wild, I payed for the whole seat but I only used half of it. This movie pulled me into North Carolina and that’s what every movie needs there audience to feel. That’s what makes this film so special.
