Game of Thrones (2011–2019)

Directed by David Benioff and D.B. Weiss

George R.R. Martin's best-selling book series "A Song of Ice and Fire" is brought to the screen as HBO sinks its considerable storytelling teeth into the medieval fantasy epic. It's the depiction of two powerful families—kings and queens, knights and renegades, liars and honest men—playing a deadly game for control of the Seven Kingdoms of Westeros and to sit atop the Iron Throne. Martin is credited as a co-executive producer and one of the writers for the series, whose shooting locations include Northern Ireland, Malta, Croatia, and Spain.


Starring

Emilia Clarke


Peter Dinklage


Kit Harington


Review

Game of Thrones has its flaws, but it can't be denied that it's one of the most watched, popular, and beloved shows on HBO. The production design alone still holds up and will most likely always do so. The cinematography is so gorgeous, the VFX is superb, there are unforgettable battles, brutal moments, and heartwarming moments—Game of Thrones has it all. Game of Thrones is a fantasy series, but it can also be described as fantasy realism with gritty and medieval elements. With a vast cast of characters, Game of Thrones juggles its characters so impressively well. Game of Thrones not only left an indelible mark on the television world, but it also paved the way for other Westeros-themed series to succeed, and it is unquestionably a must-see.
